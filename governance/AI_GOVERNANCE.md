# AI Governance Principles

StatsAgent is designed as an AI-assisted analytical system, not a fully autonomous decision-making system.

## Core Principles

- AI outputs are assistive, not authoritative.
- Statistical conclusions require validation.
- Descriptive analysis must not imply causality.
- All data transformations should be logged.
- High-impact analytical decisions require human review.
- The system should clearly separate facts, interpretation, assumptions, and limitations.

## Intended Use

StatsAgent supports:
- exploratory analysis
- descriptive statistics
- segmentation
- validation-aware reporting
- analytical reasoning support

StatsAgent does not independently:
- approve business decisions
- make financial or credit decisions
- replace human analytical judgment
- guarantee regulatory compliance

---

# Regulatory & Governance References

The governance approach used in StatsAgent is informed by publicly available AI governance and data protection frameworks, including:

- European Union Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act)
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- Human oversight and transparency principles commonly used in responsible AI guidance
- Risk-based governance approaches for high-impact analytical systems

The project is not presented as legally certified or formally compliant with any regulation.

Instead, the goal is to apply governance-oriented engineering principles such as:
- transparency
- auditability
- traceability
- human oversight
- data minimization
- responsible interpretation
